Research

Harvard School of Dental Medicine

Working with Dr. Ang Cui and her lab, I launched and refined the Immune Dictionary. The Immune Dictionary is a compilation of the gene expression of thousands of genes after exposing specific cells to 86 distinct cytokines one by one. This data is then analyzed and outputted into many graphs and tools for analysis.

The website is built in R with Shiny and launched through ShinyProxy on a virtual machine. The graphing is done through ggplot and Plotly so that graphs are more organized and are available for download.

Keck School of Medicine of USC

Eustachian tube dysfunction (ETD) is a difficult disorder to pinpoint and diagnose. However, there are ways to narrow the possibility of the disorder. With Dr. Karolina Charaziak, we work with 100+ elderly patients to determine whether or not they have eustachian tube dysfunction through surveys and William's Test, which tests the pressure of the ear itself.

Since all of the data is on a different computer in the clinic which contains personal data, it gets difficult to analyze the data. I created scripts to de-identify, extract, and reupload the data onto RedCap. This data then gets analyzed to determine what is an efficient method of diagnosing ETD.
